About Us

We are Liwach.

We are an Addis Ababa-based company focused on swapping materials to make them abundantly available to everyone in Ethiopia, That's the main goal. Our kick-off project being focused on books, we want to express how much we want to fill the gap between readers and books. We see that paperback culture is being less of a reality and pirating has been the new way of supplying oneself. We see that there is a very unreal price tag, especially on English books and unavailablity as well of some books of that kind. So understandibly, people have chosen easy digitally pirated books. But we want to change that as it hurts authors and discourage creativity. We want to help Ethiopians get any book with a price tag of 1 birr when they donate their already-read books.

We encourage anyone to buy books when they can afford to. It's worth it. But we just want to convey a message that "don't feel discouraged that you can't get your hands on a book you have your eyes on when you don't have money. Also, don't spend all your money on books when you can share books with a community. Help and let others help you. That's the Ethiopian spirit. That's what our "Equb" and "Edir" do. It's not basically a donation because somebody can't afford to lead hes/ her life in a healthy way, it's just that basic principle that humans need each other, something you don't have, somebody has and vise versa. That is the foundation our service is built upon.

We, at Liwach Books want to help redirect the reading practice of our Ethiopians. We know that we have many readers and some don't read only because they don't have access to books. ACCESS is at the core of our service and thus we work hard to make availablility a reality. Now, we don't believe somebody is not bright just because they don't read, but we want to stress on the point that reading improves visualization and problem solving ability and probably the most efficient way to grasp knowledge about some matter.

Have a good read, have a good day!

We make it easy and affordable for Ethiopians to get access to any reading material.
